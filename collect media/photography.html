Hello,
I'm Dorotea Bajic

my projects > photography

Before I was aware that packaging was a potential field of interest I was confident that I would be doing something within advertising, coming up with the concepts to communicate a message. I found it really fascinating how the creators calculate their designs to influence the viewer. My interest in photography started from social media, the most calculating medium of them all

In early 2015 I started my book blogging instagram, I spent most of my time reading then and through book clubs on goodreads I discovered #bookstagram and its aesthetic reading marvels. A few months later I was enrolled in my first photography class, one of three visual art courses my highschool offered, and I took them all and honed my skills with my bookish photos.

Everyone has a teacher that has shaped them in unforeseen ways, in high school for me was Mrs. Papak. She introduced me to photoshop, composition, lighting and other visual elements that would forever shift how i perceive.

I became so excited to practise and learn more that within a few months I was teaching her tricks in photoshop. Mrs.Papak was one of the first to curate a space I felt free to be wholly myself and create in, she encouraged me to explore and express (and still does!). My mind was ecstatic in that eclectic classroom. She had a rack of old scissors and an Emily Strange poster, both symbols holding strong in my personal aesthetic.

We really focused on the basic principles and elements, and we could really submit anything as long as we communicated how we could describe where that piece plays in our work, enabling us to delve into personal styles and exploration proving ideas on how to make it an actuality and I soon found myself executing multiple ideas and projects simultaneously, sometimes compiling into one shoot to use for a few weeks of assignments and trying other creations inbetween.

Scanography was one of the assignments and I really fell in love with the medium with its contrast and sense of depth, ease of publication and accessibility in use. You found a photocopier aka horizontal photobooth and some knicknacks? Time for some quick selfies. I find myself going to the office printer, imagine im in a sitcom and let some energy out in this instant gratification medium. The page comes out and I look into myself and previous scans come to vision in my mind archives and I ponder to when I first picked this up.

2016-Now

I spent a lot of time in that class exploring the studio and retired darkroom. There were so many old cameras in there which the students used to use, I was obsessed with taking these apart, cleaning out where the batteries leaked and learning how mechanical photography functioned. My interest led to me taking six of these cameras home (only three work) along with an Emily Strange sweater she found in her garage (my style still is greatly influenced by my obsession with this mall goth character)

Mrs. Papak even inspired me to have photography at Toronto MetU as my 2nd choice program, following in her footsteps as she once went there. But she encouraged me to make my own way too, so GCM was 1st as my calling to packaging was so strong.

My passion for all things print and packaging really took over, I stopped updating my bookstagram a few months into the program. I didn't really get the chance to pick up and play with a camera until 2020 when I was enrolled into a photography course as a part of my German exchange program. If you haven't calculated from the date already, then let me tell you myself that yeah that exchange didn't really happen.

I was intended to do a very hands- on course so for the last minute transition to a virtual semester I was enrolled into my third choice program, a full circle moment back into Advertising. The photography classes quickly became my favourite as the teacher curated similar phenomenal vibes, providing us with an outlet for the frustration we were all going through. Another pleasant surprise from the program shift was another course in web design, providing me with the foundation of skills which allowed me to create this website.

Two years after graduation, I have yet to encounter someone else with a Bachelors in Technology without being from the same program. Looking back, I was always more interested in the technical details of creation rather than the creation itself.

While I wasn't composing photos from youth, I sure did pick apart every camera my parents had, trying out all the functions, playing with the zoom and motion, testing their limits. Much like when my campus got a new printer with greater capacities and I tested the mediums we could work with to help create accurate prototypes. Just some of the many reasons why a B.Tech suits me so well

Catfish

2020

With the prompt 'Catfish', I was inspired by the elements women often exaggerate when they catfish online. I took these elements to the extreme using photoshop and a vivid colour palette that delivers an unsettling effect.



Quarantine Routine

2020

One of the first projects on my exchange photography course was to photograph a series inspired by COVID. This was in May when most accepted that this was the new reality and daily routines specific to the era were established. Using multiple exposures I wanted to showcase this repetition of steps that may be the only things that gets us through that day, and onto the next. Subjects are myself and friends.
