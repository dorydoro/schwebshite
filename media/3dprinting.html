3D Printing

2019-


 
My introduction to 3D printing was during a summer intensive course at GCM. This format really allowed me to give the course all my attention which led to a strong understanding of 3D printing technologies and strategies of creation. Features here are some of the prints I have.
 
The dancing star is the first model I have created from scratch. I used varying depths of the shapes to convey the 2D sketch in 3D and maintain the character of the illustration. To the right of that is a 3D printed scan of myself doing the ‘whoa’. Since it's all one colour it was important to create details using form, like choosing a denim button dress which would have defined rigid curves prime for handheld scanning.
 
More recently I have been focused on technical creativity, which has led me to create the teal fuzzy teletubby which was altered to collect and hold the priming lines created by the Ender 3 printer I was using at the time. It makes for cute desk decoration where I can also track my growth. The reaper teletubby was not printed by me, but through it I got to learn about SLS printing.
 
Currently I am In the middle of my hedgehog project. I sculpted the hedgehog using Meshmixer and watching the entire Sex In The City film series (I'm a Samantha). I taught myself Fusion to engineer the inner workings and additional pieces. I’ll update this page soon to showcase how I intend to have the quills (or pencils) pop out of the body.



Duolingo Owl

2019

This piece was created as a final project in my 3D printing course. Inspired by the then-recent ‘Evil Duolingo’ trend, my partner and I created this shakeable owl printed using CAD and meshmixer and output using a dual extruder 3D printer using white and green PLA which was later hand painted to this final fun product.
